FCS Teaching Topics: UDL (Universal Design for Learning) Part 3 of 3

Take-Aways

UDL is an education framework and set of principles that maximizes learning opportunities for all learners (Durham College, 2019). It is based on three main principles that should be considered when designing and teaching a course:
  1. Multiple means of representation- give learners various ways of acquiring information and knowledge
  2. Multiple means of actions and expression- provide learners alternatives for demonstrating what they know
  3. Multiple means of engagement- tap into learners' interests, offer appropriate challenges and increase motivation
For the purpose of this page, we will focus on "Multiple Means of Engagement."

Why should we incorporate multiple means of engagement?

As educators, we not only need to transfer knowledge to our students, but we need provide students multiple ways of engaging with content to further understanding, synthesize what they have learned, and be able to apply their learning correctly to solve new problems. UDL focuses on reducing BARRIERS to learning.
Have you ever asked a question to students in class, only to be met with complete silence? Why does that happen? Surely there are a number of students who can answer your question. Students want to participate but they won't if they feel they will be scrutinized, met with negativity, or if answering will just make them stand out from everyone else in the class. They need to feel safe to engage in the classroom by reducing anxieties. So, how can we effectively facilitate engagement?
Methods to try:
  1. Provide a welcoming environment, set high expectations for behaviour and ensure learners understand the culture of the class if one of respect - regardless of differences.
  2. Have students collaborate and discuss possible solutions to questions with each other first and then feedback to you. This can be done in: pairs, groups or even in a team based learning approach.
  3. Digital means for student engagement! Use TopHat (https://tophat.com/), Mentimeter (https://www.mentimeter.com/), Padlet(https://padlet.com/) or even a discussion board on Brightspace. Student can write their feedback on a digital platform, then instructors can engage formatively with students. This helps reduce anxiety among students, especially in large classes and provides an equity opportunity - everyone is involved!
  4. Provide contextualized activities - make them relevant and meaningful to your learners!
  5. Create routines such as: recap last lesson, introduce learning objectives, lecture, demonstration, activity then break (for long classes) etc. the recap the lesson before dismissing the class.
  6. Feedback - make it timely, relevant, appropriate to a task and identify strategies to incorporate for future success.
